TransPathNet: A Novel Two-Stage Framework for Indoor Radio Map Prediction

Abstract

Accurate indoor pathloss prediction is crucial for optimizing wireless communication in complex environments. This paper introduces TransPathNet, a two-stage deep learning framework that utilizes a transformer-based feature extraction backbone and a multiscale convolutional attention decoder. Demonstrating state-of-the-art performance in the ICASSP 2025 Indoor Pathloss Radio Map Prediction Challenge, TransPathNet achieves an overall Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) of 10.397 on the challenge test set, demonstrating superior generalization across varied indoor geometries, frequencies, and antenna patterns.

Overview of TransPathNet Training Process. The framework employs a two-stage architecture: a coarse stage and a fine stage. TransPathNet, utilized in both stages, integrates the TransNext and EMCAD to produce robust pathloss predictions.
